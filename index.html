§ I  ·  ABOUT

A note on what I do

¶Research at the intersection of PDE analysis, continuum mechanics, and applied electromagnetism.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Instituto de Ciencias Matemáticas (ICMAT) in Madrid, where I work within the Severo Ochoa programme and the project on the dynamics of singular incompressible fluids led by Diego Córdoba.

Before arriving in Madrid, I spent three years as a postdoc with Tomas Dohnal at Martin-Luther-Universität Halle-Wittenberg, and earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics at Heriot-Watt University, Edinburgh, under the supervision of Andrew Lacey and Heiko Gimperlein.

My work centres on the rigorous analysis of nonlinear partial differential equations — in particular the interplay between elliptic, hyperbolic, parabolic, and dispersive structures that appear in models of micro-electro-mechanical systems, surface plasmon polaritons, and, more recently, fluid dynamics.

Nonlinear evolution PDEs

Wellposedness, long-time behaviour, and singularity formation.

Variational & functional analytic methods

Calculus of variations, semigroup theory, fixed-point arguments.

Continuum mechanics

Elastic deformation and gas flow, motivated by MEMS.

PDE bifurcation

Nonlinear solutions from the spectrum; PT-symmetric problems.

AI for PDEs

Physics-informed neural networks for evolutionary problems.

Fluid dynamics

Nonlocal/nonlinear models and fluid–structure interaction.
